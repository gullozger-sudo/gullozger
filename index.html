Om Gull Øzger

Jeg heter Gull Øzger. Jeg er koreograf, regissør og dansekunstner. Jeg er fra Mo i Rana i Nordland og bor i Oslo.

Jeg lager forestillinger og prosjekter — ofte utendørs, på grenser og i samarbeid med kunstnere fra mange land.

Siden 2018 jobber jeg med noe jeg kaller soft aktivisme. Det handler om å bruke kroppens mykhet og sårbarhet som politisk uttrykk — i stedet for konfrontasjon og støy.

Jeg driver GullBakken sammen med tre andre kunstnere. I arbeidet mitt er jeg opptatt av å inkludere alle — uansett alder, religion, kjønn, etnisitet, funksjonsvariasjon eller bakgrunn. Mangfold er ikke et tillegg, men en del av selve praksisen.

Jeg har nedsatt hørsel og bruker høreapparat.

About Gull Øzger

My name is Gull Øzger. I am a choreographer, director, and dance artist from Mo i Rana in Northern Norway. I live in Oslo.

I make performances and projects — often outdoors, at borders, and in collaboration with artists from many countries.

Since 2018, I have worked with a concept I call soft activism. This means using the body's softness and vulnerability as a form of political expression — instead of confrontation and noise.

I run GullBakken together with three other artists. In my work, I am committed to including everyone — regardless of age, religion, gender, ethnicity, disability, or background. Diversity is not an add-on, but part of the practice itself.

I have a hearing impairment and use a hearing aid.

Artistic Practice

Gull Øzger is a Norwegian choreographer, director, and dance artist born in Mo i Rana, Nordland. Her practice is rooted in the body as a site of political, cultural, and sensory knowledge — and in the specific landscapes, languages, and border zones of Northern Norway and the Barents region. She is not a visitor to this geography: she comes from it.

A defining strand of her practice since 2018 is what she calls soft activism — a choreographic and political approach that grew out of her work on Close(d) Border Connections. Where conventional activism is often confrontational, loud, and conflict-driven, soft activism places the body's softness, vulnerability, and physical form at the centre of political expression. The soft body in a border zone becomes the most radical statement possible.

Through GullBakken, she develops work deeply rooted in non-normative perspectives — questioning whose bodies, whose languages, and whose ways of organising are considered the norm in art and in public space. The company works with non-hierarchical collaborative structures and a deep engagement with place, operating alongside — not on behalf of — the communities and knowledge systems the work engages with.

She is committed to creating environments where difference — in age, background, ability, nationality, and culture — is the creative material, not the exception.
